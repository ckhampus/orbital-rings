### Semantic Deviation: UI-03 Display Numerator

`PopulationDisplay` shows `CitizenCount/TotalCapacity` (total population vs housing capacity) rather than `TotalHoused/TotalCapacity` (housed citizens vs capacity). The requirement text says "count/capacity" which is ambiguous. Showing total population may be the better game design choice — a station with 8 citizens and 5 housing beds showing "8/5" clearly signals the player needs more housing, while "5/5" would misleadingly suggest full coverage.

Not a wiring break or missing feature. Classified as informational tech debt.

## Tech Debt Summary

### Phase 15: HousingManager Core
- **Potential double-add in `_roomOccupants` during `RestoreFromSave`:** `InitializeExistingRooms()` calls `AssignAllUnhoused()` before the explicit foreach loop re-assigns from saved data. If assignments overlap, citizens may appear twice in occupant lists. UAT confirmed functional, but fragile under tight capacity. Severity: Warning.

### Phase 16/19: Capacity Transfer / Save-Load
- **`HousingManager.StateLoaded` not reset in `TitleScreen.StartNewStation`:** If player loads → returns to title → starts new game, `HousingManager._Ready()` would skip `InitializeExistingRooms`. Pre-existing issue, documented as out-of-scope in Phase 16 research. Severity: Low (autoloads don't re-run `_Ready` on scene change currently).

### Phase 18: Housing UI
- **Stale REQUIREMENTS.md wording:** UI-01 says `"--"` for unhoused citizens; implementation uses `"No home"` per user decision during execution. Not a gap — just stale documentation.
- **PopulationDisplay semantics:** Numerator is total citizens, not housed citizens. See integration section above.

**Total:** 4 items across 4 phases. No blockers.
